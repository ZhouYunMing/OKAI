无处不在的人工智能

Aritificial Intelligence (AI) is playing a bigger and bigger role in our lives, and there are some exciting applications of AI recently. This chapter will give you an overview of what AI is and its impact. Keep scrolling!

Scroll

Emerging around 1956, AI has made significant progress in recent years.

From autonomous driving cars, to smart supermarkets with no cashier, to algorithms that can play the game of Go and defeat human champions across the world, to an AI-generated painting that has been sold for $432,500, we have witnessed some exciting AI applications.

A lot of people believe that the Industrial Revolution has changed the world. Similarly, nowadays many predict AI will bring another revolution that affects the way we live in an equally significant way, if not more.

Unlike what is depicted in Hollywood movies and science fiction films, AI is not a big scary robot taking over the world but is actually all around you. AI applications can be subtle things that we use every day, like the algorithm that recommends a TV show to watch.

In concise words, AI is the computerization of tasks that usually require human intelligence. If you are still picturing a robot in your head, then think of AI as the brain of that robot, not the physical hardware. Just as steam engines freed humans from intense repetitive physical labor, AI is freeing us from low-level tedious brain labor.

Today’s AI is still what scientists describe as Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI). This means that a single set of algorithms can only perform a specific task - like driving, face detection, recommendation, etc.

Scientists and researchers are working towards the ultimate goal of AI - Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), where algorithms could work together to complete a variety of tasks as well as any human could.

Buzzword: Algorithm

An algorithm is very much like a cooking recipe. It is a set of clear instructions that you or a machine can follow to achieve a certain purpose. Computers do not understand vague high-level instructions that humans are used to, and algorithms help translate these instructions to machine-level operations that computers can follow.

Buzzword: Machine Learning

Machine Learning (ML) is a specific area of AI. As described by its name, machine learning aims to train a machine to learn based on data rather than pre-defined rules. After the algorithm is trained with large amounts of data, it will be able to perform tasks at a relatively high level of performance.

Buzzword: Deep Learning

Deep Learning (DL) is a branch of machine learning that centers around Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). The following chapters focus on explaining concepts in this subfield of AI. Deep learning algorithms need even more data than traditional machine learning algorithms, but are more flexible and powerful.

Summary

In this chapter, we looked at what AI is and its impact on society. It is worth noting that, although we have seen exiciting advancements of AI, we are still far from achieving AGI. In the next chapter, we are diving deeper into deep learning, the area of AI that witnessed major development in recent years.
